## What is CIFAR-10 Airbench?

CIFAR-10 Airbench contains the fastest known algorithms for training a neural network to 94% or 96% accuracy on CIFAR-10 using a single NVIDIA A100 GPU. The project provides fast and stable training baselines to help accelerate machine learning research on one of the most widely used datasets in the field.

<Info>
CIFAR-10 is one of the most widely used datasets in machine learning, facilitating thousands of research projects per year. Airbench provides easily runnable dependency-free PyTorch scripts that can replace classic baselines like training ResNet-20 or ResNet-18.
</Info>

## Performance Benchmarks

Airbench achieves state-of-the-art training speeds on CIFAR-10:

| Script | Mean Accuracy | Time | PFLOPs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| airbench94_muon.py | 94.01% | 2.59s | 0.29 |
| airbench96_faster.py | 96.00% | 27.3s | 3.1 |

<Note>
Timings are measured using `torch==2.4.1` on a 400W NVIDIA A100.
</Note>

For comparison, standard training used in most CIFAR-10 studies:

| Baseline | Mean Accuracy | Time | PFLOPs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Standard ResNet-18 | 96.0% | 7 min | 32.3 |

**That's over 160x faster than standard training!**

## How It Works

Airbench achieves these remarkable speeds through several key innovations:

- **Muon Optimizer**: A specialized optimizer for ultra-fast convergence (see [Muon Optimizer](/concepts/muon-optimizer))
- **GPU-Accelerated Data Loading**: All preprocessing happens on GPU
- **Whitening Layer**: Improves conditioning of the optimization problem
- **Efficient Architecture**: Carefully designed ConvNet with optimal width and depth
- **Data Filtering**: Selective training on informative examples

<Tip>
The methods used to obtain these training speeds are described in detail in [the paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.00498).
</Tip>

## Use Cases

Airbench is perfect for:

- **Fast Experimentation**: Iterate quickly on new ideas with 2-30 second training cycles
- **Data Selection**: Use as a platform for active learning and data filtering experiments
- **Baseline Comparisons**: Replace slow ResNet baselines with fast, modern alternatives
- **Educational Projects**: Learn deep learning without waiting hours for training
- **Research Prototyping**: Test hypotheses rapidly before scaling to larger datasets
